# Official Exam Formats

## TOEIC Listening & Reading

### Reading Section (100 questions, 75 minutes)

**Part 5: Incomplete Sentences** (30 questions)
- Fill grammar/vocabulary gaps from 4 choices
- Example: "The company ____ a new policy." (A) implement (B) implements (C) implementing (D) implemented

**Part 6: Text Completion** (16 questions)
- Complete passages (emails, articles) with suitable words/sentences
- Includes sentence insertion for organization

**Part 7: Reading Comprehension** (54 questions)
- Single passages (29 questions)
- Multiple passages (25 questions)
- Tests: vocabulary, inference, main idea, writer's intent

### Listening Section (100 questions, 45 minutes)

**Part 1: Photographs** (6 questions)
- Describe images from 4 statements

**Part 2: Question-Response** (25 questions)
- Choose best response to question/statement from 3 options

**Part 3: Conversations** (39 questions)
- 13 conversations, 3 questions each
- 2-3 speakers in workplace settings

**Part 4: Short Talks** (30 questions)
- 10 talks, 3 questions each
- Announcements, ads, instructions

## IELTS Academic

### Reading (60 minutes, 40 questions)

**Question Types:**
1. **Matching** (5 types):
   - Headings to paragraphs
   - Information to paragraphs
   - Features to descriptions
   - Sentence endings
   - Names/theories

2. **Completion Tasks**:
   - Sentence completion
   - Summary completion
   - Note completion
   - Table completion
   - Flow-chart completion
   - Short answer questions (word limit)

3. **True/False/Not Given**:
   - Identifying information

4. **Yes/No/Not Given**:
   - Identifying writer's views/claims

5. **Multiple Choice**:
   - Single answer
   - Multiple answers

### Listening (30 minutes + 10 transfer, 40 questions)

**Question Types:**
- Multiple choice
- Matching
- Form/sentence/note completion
- Diagram/map/plan labelling

### Writing (60 minutes, 2 tasks)

**Task 1** (150 words, 20 minutes):
- Describe/summarise visual data
- Graph, table, chart, diagram, process, map

**Task 2** (250 words, 40 minutes):
- Formal essay
- Respond to viewpoint/argument/problem with examples

### Speaking (11-14 minutes)

**Part 1** (4-5 minutes):
- Introduction and personal questions

**Part 2** (3-4 minutes):
- 2-minute monologue from cue card
- 1 minute preparation

**Part 3** (4-5 minutes):
- Discussion on abstract topics related to Part 2
